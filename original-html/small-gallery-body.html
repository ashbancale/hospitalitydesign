    1. The “Try On Truck”
    This is True & Co.’s first go into physical retail space, with plans to take their mobile retail store traveling across the country for custom bra fittings. Think Bento box/Origami meets transformable architecture on wheels, inspired by the “tiny house movement.” (Designed by Spiegel Aihara Workshop.)

  • Photo by DAVID BROWN (imagery also appeared in Green Living Magazine November 2014)

    Print

    2. The Mobile Supermarket
    “Tokushimaru”—grocery shopping to your front door—is growing where Amazon or Fresh Direct is not widely known or used (such as rural towns in Japan, primarily with elderly residents). In parts of the United States, mobile food markets travel to lower income neighborhoods that do not have readily available close markets to access fresh produce and healthy foods.
     

  • Photo by FRANCIS DZIKOWSKI

    Print

    3. The New Stand
    Today’s consumer is constantly on the go and always connected. As consumers go about their daily commute, “The New Stand” answers the innovative need of convenience. Log onto their app while on the train to place an order for your daily essentials, be introduced to new products and have your order ready when you get off at that stop.
