    Visual design students from FIDM (Fashion Institute of Design & Merchandising) recently participated in a design challenge at a Los Angeles 99 Cents Only Stores. Four groups of three students were challenged with creating a one-of-a-kind high fashion design using items purchased from 99 Cents Only Stores. Each group selected a theme at random based on the departments in the store. The themes included Household Supplies, Halloween Products, Food Packaging and Party Supplies. The finished designs were displayed in the store and voted on by customers.

  • Photos by Benjamin Shmikler/ABImages

    Print

    The gown constructed out of household supplies by Jordyn Lowe, Devyn Moore and Rachel Moler was voted “Customer Favorite” by the 99 Cents Only Store customers.

    Materials used include: kitchen sponges, dish towels, feather dusters, coffee filters, faux flowers and Wonder Puffs facial sponges.
